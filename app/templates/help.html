Help

How to put {{ site_title }} on your phone, connect your Substack, and keep episodes for flights.

📱 Add to your iPhone

  1. Open {{ request.url.hostname }} in Safari (it has to be Safari — in-app browsers like the ones inside Discord or Instagram can't install it) and log in.
  2. Tap the Share button — the square with the arrow pointing up, in the bar at the bottom.
  3. Scroll down the share sheet and tap Add to Home Screen, then Add.
  4. Launch it from the new home-screen icon — it opens full screen like a real app.

The installed app keeps its own login, so you'll sign in once more the first time you open it. Your listening positions follow you automatically — they sync through your account, not the browser.

🤖 Add to Android

  1. Open {{ request.url.hostname }} in Chrome and log in.
  2. Tap the ⋮ menu (top-right).
  3. Tap Add to Home screen — on some phones it says Install app. Confirm.

🍪 Connect your Substack (get your cookie)

Connecting your Substack account is what lets {{ site_title }} mirror your subscriptions. It works by borrowing your Substack login cookie — a long code your browser holds after you sign in. Grab it on a computer:

  1. In Chrome (or Firefox), go to substack.com and make sure you're logged in.
  2. Press F12 (or right-click the page → Inspect) to open the developer tools.
  3. Chrome: open the Application tab → under Storage choose Cookies → https://substack.com.
    Firefox: it's the Storage tab → Cookies.
  4. In the table, find the row named substack.sid. Double-click its Value, select all of it (it's long, often starting with s%3A), and copy.
  5. Back here, open Accounts → paste the cookie, enter your Substack @handle, and hit Connect.

Treat the cookie like a password. Anyone holding it can use your Substack account. Only ever paste it into the Accounts page here. If it ever leaks, log out of Substack in that browser (or sign out of all sessions in Substack's settings) — that instantly kills the old cookie — then reconnect a fresh one.

After copying: just close the tab. Don't log out of substack.com in that browser — logging out invalidates the cookie and your syncing stops until you reconnect.

Make your reading list public. In your Substack profile settings, set your reads/subscriptions to visible — that's how your full subscription list gets discovered. If it's private, only a partial list syncs; you can always add missing publications by URL on the Accounts page.

✈️ Keep episodes for offline

  1. Open any episode and tap 📦 Save for offline.
  2. Find everything you've saved under 📦 Saved in the header — it works with no connection at all (airplane mode included).
  3. Saved audio survives updates and stays until you remove it (🗑 Remove offline copy on the episode page).

Save episodes while you still have internet — at home before the flight, not at the gate.
